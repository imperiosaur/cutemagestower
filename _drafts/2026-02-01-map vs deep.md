Unlocking in puzzle hunts is a thankless topic. When it works well, no one notices. When it works badly, it becomes everyone's focus. Let's take a moment to talk about why.


# The Importance of Unlocking

Before we dig into the concept of unlocking mechanisms, it's important to state why they are important to have in the first place. After all, if something only causes problems, why would we want to have it in the first place? It turns out that there are a couple really important reasons to have unlocking mechanisms and it's worth mentioning them before we dive into the specifics.

* **They keep the solvers from getting overwhelmed.** The 2026 MIT Mystery Hunt lists 232 solvable puzzles on its [:All Puzzles Page](#AllPuzzles). Imagine if all of them opened at once. Solvers would get quickly overwhelmed by choice and it would be tough to build up an intuitive understanding of the Hunt. By starting small and unlocking piecemeal, solvers can slowly build up a better understanding of the Hunt over time, giving them more time to understand individual plot beats.
* **They keep the running team from getting overwhelmed.** If everything opens at the same time, that means that every physical puzzle opens up at the same time and every interaction opens [:at the same time](#2018Opening). This can cause a logistical nightmare for running teams as tons of manpower is needed at once. By staggering when these open up, the logistics folks can spread out the things they need to do over the course of the weekend.
* **They allow for a story and a sense of progression.** 
* **They provide a restriction that can be used creatively.** As Mark Rosewater has often stated "Restrictions breed creativity." The Terminus round from the 2026 Hunt is a perfect example of this. The Terminus round used the fact that you needed to figure out how to unlock the puzzles as a key part of the structure and the meta. If everything opened at the beginning, this round couldn't exist.
* **They are the vehicle used to maintain puzzle radius.** Puzzle radius is a concept for [:another blog](#Charts), but a quick explanation is important. Puzzle radius is the number of puzzles that a team has open at one time
