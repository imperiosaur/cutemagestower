Five by Five


Five-pointed stars (unlike the more common round ones) always collapse in mere days. Each point decays in its own way, changing shape 4–8 times in one of 26 standardized patterns, as determined by its type; for example, a point of type K will change shape four times, to a K, an I, an L, and finally an O. In any given star, all the points are of different type (so we can number the points I–V in alphabetical order by type) and exactly one point will change shape each day; this may or may not be visible to optical telescopes depending on seeing conditions. For instance, in the following example...
